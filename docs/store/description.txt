Decant converts documents to Markdown before they upload to an LLM chat —
locally, in your browser, without interrupting how you already work.


THE PROBLEM

Uploading a PDF to an LLM is often the most expensive way to share it. Many chat
backends render every page as an image and bill you for that alongside the text,
so a document whose actual content is modest can balloon in cost.

Converting to Markdown first cuts that cost, keeps headings, lists, and tables
intact, and — because the conversion happens on your own machine — never hands
your document to a third-party conversion service.


HOW IT WORKS

Attach a file the way you always do: the paperclip, drag-and-drop, or paste.
Decant catches it on the way out, converts it, and swaps in the Markdown version
in place. You get a .md attachment instead of a 15 MB PDF.

No extra steps. No separate app to open. No copy-paste dance.


SUPPORTED FORMATS

• PDF (digital / text-based)
• Word — .docx
• Excel — .xlsx, .xls
• PowerPoint — .pptx
• HTML — saved web pages

Charts in Office files are recovered as data tables rather than lost as pictures.


WHERE IT RUNS

Enabled out of the box: claude.ai, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity.
Add them yourself in options: Microsoft Copilot, kimi.com.

claude.ai, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Copilot get full coverage — file picker,
drag-and-drop, and paste. Gemini and kimi.com convert through the file picker;
their uploaders reject synthetic drops, so on those two, drag-and-drop and paste
deliberately send your original file through unchanged rather than lose it, and
say so on screen.


IT WON'T QUIETLY WRECK YOUR DOCUMENT

The image layer isn't pure waste — it's what lets a model read charts, scans, and
complex tables. So Decant never converts blindly:

• Scanned and image-only PDFs pass through untouched. No prompt, no degradation.
• Documents mixing text with real charts or photos ask you first, offering three
  choices: convert to Markdown, convert and attach the figures alongside it, or
  send the original.
• A hotkey (Alt+Shift+O) forces the next upload through untouched.
• Anything Decant doesn't handle uploads exactly as it would if the extension
  weren't installed.


PRIVACY

• Conversion happens in your browser. Decant operates no servers.
• No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking, no data sold or shared.
• Default-deny: Decant does nothing on any site until you enable that site
  yourself, and enabling one asks Chrome for permission to run there.
• Settings sync through Chrome's own storage, like your bookmarks. They never
  reach a Decant server, because there isn't one.

Full policy:
https://github.com/jtrachtenberg/decant/blob/main/docs/privacy.md


WHAT TO EXPECT

Across six real-world PDFs — annual reports, government FAQs, financial guides —
conversion cut estimated token cost by roughly a third to a half, and file size
by 60–99%. Several 15 MB print-production PDFs became under 160 KB of Markdown.
Each converted in under about three seconds.

Token figures are estimates: actual billing depends on the model and backend, and
savings shrink on figure-heavy documents where you choose to attach the visuals.
The size reductions are measured. Full methodology and per-document numbers are
in the project README.


OPTIONAL: THE HIGHER-FIDELITY TIER

For scanned documents needing OCR, or image-heavy files you'd rather not
simplify, Decant can hand off to an optional companion service you run on your
own machine. It talks to localhost only — nothing leaves your computer. Entirely
opt-in, and the extension is fully useful without it.


CONFIGURABLE

• Which sites Decant runs on
• What happens per file type — convert, pass through, or route to your own
  endpoint
• The passthrough hotkey
• A default answer for the convert-or-not prompt, so it stops asking
